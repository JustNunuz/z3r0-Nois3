TITLE: Shona RockYou: Decoding Cultural Entropy and the Architecture of Predictability

---

### Introduction: The Myth of Randomness

In the world of cybersecurity, the name "RockYou" is synonymous with one of the most significant password leaks in history. It served as a wakeup call about the predictability of human choices. However, for the SADC region and speakers of Bantu languages like Shona, predictability is not just about common numbers or special characters; it is deeply rooted in **cultural and linguistic architecture**.

"Shona RockYou" is a research project that ethically repurposes the concept of lexical pattern analysis to study human-generated linguistic strings—not credentials, but the meaningful patterns that define our digital identity.

### The Problem: The "False Security" Trap

As a Certified Data Protection Officer (DPO), I have observed a critical gap in our security infrastructure: **Western-centric entropy models**.

Standard password strength meters and security audits calculate "randomness" based on character frequency and English-language templates. These tools often fail to account for the structural regularity of Shona. A Shona speaker might create a string that feels complex to a Western algorithm, yet follows a rigid cultural template, creating a "False Security" trap.

### The Research: Mapping Shona Linguistic Slots

Shona names, surnames, and totems are semantically rich and structurally predictable. They often follow a system of **morphological slots**. For instance, many common given names are built on repeating roots:

- **-ashe** (God/Grace): *Tinashe, Munashe, Kudakwashe*
- **-enda** (Thanks): *Tendai, Tatenda, Anesu*
- **-wa** (Bestowed): *Tapiwa, Kupakwashe*

When these patterns are combined with cultural naming conventions (such as using totems/mitupo like *Soko* or *Moyo*), the mathematical entropy of the resulting string is significantly lower than a generic "random" calculator would suggest.

### Computational Forensics: The Mutation Engine

To study this phenomenon, we developed the **Shona Leet Mutator** (`shona_leet_mutator.py`). This tool algorithmically generates morphological and "leet-speak" variants of Shona lexical items. By analyzing the resulting search space, we can:

1. **Quantify Cultural Entropy:** Measure exactly how much "shorter" the search path is when an auditor understands Shona linguistic structure.
2. **Predictable Mutations:** Map how users likely transform Shona vowels (a, e, i, o, u) into symbolic substitutes—a process that differs from English leet-speak due to Shona's vowel-final phonology.
3. **Audit Readiness:** Provide regional DPOs and security researchers with the wordlists (`tweets.txt`, `combined_raw.txt`) needed to perform culturally aware security audits.

### Beyond Security: Documentation and Preservation

While started as a security research project, Shona RockYou has larger implications for **Computational Linguistics**. By building structured wordlists of given names, surnames, and place names, we are creating a digital corpus that preserves Shona's linguistic richness while providing the data necessary to improve NLP models for the region.

The project sits at the intersection of security and sovereignty. It is about ensuring that our digital protections are as linguistically informed as the people they are designed to protect.
